Design Process

Target Users

  • Primary Users: Individuals with red-green, blue-yellow, or total color blindness.
  • Common Use Cases: Students, professionals, designers, and casual computer users.
  • Other Users: UI/UX designers, developers, and accessibility advocates.
  • Participant Selection: Colorblind individuals who regularly use digital devices for work, school, or personal tasks.

Primary Design Goals

  • Goal 1: Real-Time Color Adjustment Tool – Provides instant contrast and color modifications for digital interfaces.
  • Goal 2: Customizable Accessibility Profiles – Users can create and save personalized color settings for different applications.
  • Goal 3: Collaborative User Feedback System – Allows users to share accessibility settings and recommendations with a community.

User Research

We used a mixed method approach combining contextual inquiry and semi-structured interviews to gain a deeper understanding of user challenges and needs:

  • Contextual Inquiry: Observed how users with various types of color blindness interacted with the app, including time spent on tasks like interpreting Ishihara plates.
  • Semi-Structured Interviews: Asked participants what they found frustrating, their preferences, and suggestions for improvements in color contrast settings and accessibility features.

This approach provided both behavioral observations and personal insights to inform design decisions.
